Particulate Matter (PM10 and PM2.5)

Sources: Construction sites, unpaved roads, smokestacks, fires, vehicle emissions.

Harmful Effects: Respiratory and cardiovascular problems, lung cancer, premature death, environmental degradation.

Safe Limits: WHO: PM2.5 - 24-hour mean of 15 µg/m³, annual mean of 5 µg/m³; PM10 - 24-hour mean of 45 µg/m³, annual mean of 15 µg/m³; EPA: PM2.5 - 24-hour average of 35 µg/m³, annual average of 12 µg/m³; PM10 - 24-hour average of 150 µg/m³.

Nitrogen Dioxide (NO₂)

Sources: Vehicle emissions, power plants, industrial activities.

Harmful Effects: Respiratory issues, reduced lung function, increased susceptibility to respiratory infections, contributes to ground-level ozone and particulate matter formation.

Safe Limits: WHO: 1-hour mean of 200 µg/m³; EPA: Annual average of 53 ppb, 1-hour average of 100 ppb.

Ozone (O₃)

Sources: Formed by the reaction of sunlight with pollutants such as VOCs and NOx.

Harmful Effects: Respiratory problems, exacerbation of asthma, reduced lung function, harmful to vegetation and ecosystems.

Safe Limits: WHO: 8-hour mean of 100 µg/m³; EPA: 8-hour average of 70 ppb.

Sulfur Dioxide (SO₂)

Sources: Burning of coal and oil, industrial processes, volcanic eruptions.

Harmful Effects: Respiratory problems, aggravation of heart and lung diseases, formation of acid rain.

Safe Limits: WHO: 24-hour mean of 20 µg/m³; EPA: 1-hour average of 75 ppb (parts per billion).
